The Israel-Iran conflict: Pots, Pans and Knives.

Published on March 29, 2026 | By Sarthak Bhattacharyya (Notes from Sanjay Rath)

From the profound analytical lens of Pt. Sanjay Rath (srath.com), the geopolitical tensions in the Middle East—specifically between Israel and Iran—carry significant astrological markers that reflect the deeper cosmic rhythms of our time.

Suspicions under Rāhu

In a recent analysis, Pt. Sanjay Rath metaphorically describes the two nations involved as "fine ladies." Israel is identified with Karkaṭa rāśi (Cancer), the sign ruled by the Moon, representing nurturing and home. Iran is associated with Vṛṣabha rāśi (Taurus), ruled by Venus, representing stability and values.

While Cancer and Taurus share a harmonious 3/11 relationship (typically conducive to cooperation), the intrusion of Rāhu—the master of illusion and suspicion—has poisoned the well. Under the antardasa of Rahu, these "ladies" have begun to view each other through a distorted lens of threat and hostility.

Israel (Karkaṭa)

Under Lunar influence, focusing on preservation and defense of the 'Home'.

Iran (Vṛṣabha)

Under Venusian influence, focusing on value systems and deep-rooted stability.

The Shadow (Rāhu)

Introducing paranoia, suspicion, and the drive for unconventional aggression.

The Entry of 'Big Daddy'

The situation escalates with the involvement of external powers. Sanjay Rath refers to the United States as "Big Daddy," associated with Kumbha (Aquarius). Aquarius, being an air sign ruled by Saturn and Rahu, enters the fray with a volatile temper.

The result, as described in Rath's allegorical style, is that the "Venusian" figure (Iran) ends up with "pots, pans, and knives thrown at her," leading to significant turmoil and a breakdown of the initial compatible relationship.

Predictive Boundaries

Interestingly, Pt. Sanjay Rath notes a shift in his own practice: "I have stopped making those predictions – I just enjoy following jyotiṣa." This highlights a crucial aspect of Vedic Wisdom—observation and understanding of the cosmic play (Lila) are often more valuable than mere speculative forecasting.

"The planets reflect the minds of the people and the collective karma of nations. When Rahu clouds the vision, even friends see enemies."
